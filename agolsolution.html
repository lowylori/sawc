ArcGIS Online Solution

For this solution, we adapted part of the Park and Grounds Managament pre-ade solutions, while adding a reporting components. The major components we used for this solutions were: 2 Surveys, a dashboard, a management center and a reporting system

Surveys

The first component of this solution was the surveys to collect the request and relavent information. There were two surveys designed for this: one internal and one external. The internal survey was designed for staff to go and inspect an area of peice of equipment. A photo of it here: (add photo-AGOL_Internal). This would require a staff memember to be logged into their AGOL account, and it would automatically log their name and email.

The external survey was designed for anyone to fill in. It has a reduced number of select options to report an incident and gives the user the option to add in their contact details.

Both of these surveys then had an accomapning feature layer in ArcGIS Online, as well as they were combined into one feature layer: Requests.

A downfall with the surveys was that they were already published when deploying the solution. This made editing them difficult. If given more time, new surveys with new questions would need to be made to match a clients need. Adding questions to the exisitng survey and republishing it caused an error.

Managment Center

The data from the surveys were also linked to the managment center. This is where an admin or operations lead would log in and assign the open tasks to an employee to either inspect or fix. Upon the home page, you are given the option to see the open requests (manage requests) or see the unassigned work (manage assignments). Link it here: https://experience.arcgis.com/experience/659b8dce9c284f0ba819f52dd331ec70

A photo of it here: (add photo-AGOL_ManagmentCenter).

Unfortunatly, this is good in theory but the Manage requests button forms an error now. The program would sometimes work but sometimes provide an error.

Request Manager

The request is a dashboard that provides a snapshot of open requests, who is assigned to them and any relevant details of the request. Managers are able to click on specific requests in the table and add more details to them if needed. Link it here: https://fleming.maps.arcgis.com/apps/dashboards/f103cfd878e44aaeb3207de35781109e

Request Dashboard

The request is a dashboard that provides a snapshot of open requests, where they are on the map, the request type and when the requests were submitted. This is a good visual aid to see how many requests are still open and in what categories are they being reported. This is linked in the Management center, on the navigation bar, under requests and monitor. https://fleming.maps.arcgis.com/apps/dashboards/f103cfd878e44aaeb3207de35781109e

The downside here is I was not able to change the default map extent from my location to the location of where your requests are. You have to search the location of requests in the map extent for the dashboard to show the relevant information

Overall Thoughts

The AGOL solution overall is well designed and would meet the needs of our problem statement. A few issues that arose while working with this solution included the surveys being difficult to add new questions to, as well as the Managment Dashboard being unreliable. The Managment Dashboard would often have issues connecting to the data, but work other times. For an overall solution, this information could be acessed other places so it would not be the a big down fall of the solution.
